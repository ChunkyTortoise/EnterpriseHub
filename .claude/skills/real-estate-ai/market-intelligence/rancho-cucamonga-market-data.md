# Skill: Rancho Cucamonga Market Intelligence & Data Context

## Overview
This skill provides deep contextual knowledge of the Rancho Cucamonga, California real estate market, enabling Claude to interpret market data with local nuance.

## Core Market Regions

### 1. Alta Loma (Premium Foothills)
- **91701/91737**: Foothills lots, equestrian properties, panoramic views. Market driven by families seeking space and schools.
- Premium for elevation and view lots. Older ranch-style homes being redeveloped into modern estates.
- Proximity to hiking trails (Cucamonga Peak, Sapphire Falls).

### 2. Etiwanda (Family Hub)
- **91739**: Top-rated Etiwanda School District. Highest family demand in the Inland Empire.
- Newer planned communities with parks, trails, and community centers.
- Strong appreciation driven by school ratings and safety perception.

### 3. Central Rancho Cucamonga
- **91730**: Haven Avenue corridor, Victoria Gardens proximity, mixed-use development.
- Foothill Boulevard retail strip, Metrolink station walkability.
- Entry-level to mid-range pricing, strong rental demand from commuters.

### 4. Terra Vista / Day Creek
- **91730/91739**: Master-planned communities, HOA-managed, newer construction.
- Day Creek area seeing significant new development and commercial growth.
- Popular with move-up buyers seeking modern amenities.

## Economic Drivers
- **Logistics/Warehousing**: Ontario/Rancho corridor is a major distribution hub (Amazon, FedEx, UPS).
- **Healthcare**: San Antonio Regional Hospital, Kaiser proximity driving professional housing demand.
- **LA Commuter Corridor**: Metrolink + I-10/I-15 access makes RC attractive for LA County workers seeking affordability.
- **Victoria Gardens**: Major retail/entertainment center driving local economic activity.

## Market-Specific Nuances
- **Property Taxes**: California Prop 13 limits reassessment, so newer purchases carry higher effective rates (~1.1-1.3%).
- **Fire Zone Awareness**: Foothills properties may be in Very High Fire Hazard Severity Zones (VHFHSZ). Insurance costs vary significantly.
- **Mello-Roos**: Many newer developments carry Mello-Roos special tax districts. Always factor into affordability.
- **Seasonal Flux**: Market peaks Spring/Summer (school year timing). Winter slowdown less pronounced than colder markets.
